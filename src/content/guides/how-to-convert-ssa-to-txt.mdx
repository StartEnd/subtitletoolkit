SSA files are useful as old SubStation Alpha subtitle projects, but they are noisy when you only need the words. A raw `.ssa` file mixes dialogue with script metadata, style definitions, timing fields, margins, effects, and sometimes override tags.

Converting SSA to TXT creates a readable transcript copy for review, translation, quoting, notes, or content reuse.

## Quick answer

Use the [SSA to TXT Converter](/tools/ssa-to-txt/) to convert SSA subtitles to plain text. The tool accepts `.ssa` files, extracts `Dialogue:` text, removes timing and styling metadata, converts SSA line breaks, and keeps processing local in your browser.

Keep the original SSA file if you still need timed subtitles. The TXT output is for reading and editing, not playback.

## When to convert SSA to TXT

Convert SSA to TXT when:

- a translator only needs dialogue text
- a reviewer should not see timing fields or style syntax
- an old SSA file needs a transcript draft
- you want quotes or notes from a subtitle file
- you need searchable text from a styled subtitle project
- the original file has SSA style sections that make manual copying slow

If the destination still needs timed captions, use [SSA to SRT](/guides/how-to-convert-ssa-to-srt/) or [SSA to VTT](/guides/how-to-convert-ssa-to-vtt/) instead.

## What gets removed

SSA to TXT conversion removes the subtitle structure and keeps the readable dialogue:

- `[Script Info]`, `[V4 Styles]`, and `[Events]` section headers
- `Format:` rows that define SSA field order
- `Dialogue:` timing, style, margin, name, and effect fields
- `Comment:` rows that are not delivery captions
- style definitions for fonts, colors, borders, shadows, and alignment
- SSA override tags such as `{\an8}`, `{\pos(x,y)}`, or `{\c&H...&}`
- hard line break codes such as `\N`

The result is plain text, usually with blank lines between subtitle events.

## Step-by-step workflow

1. Save a copy of the original `.ssa` file.
2. Open the [SSA to TXT Converter](/tools/ssa-to-txt/).
3. Upload or paste the SSA subtitle content.
4. Convert the file to TXT.
5. Review the output for missing dialogue, excessive line breaks, and garbled characters.
6. Copy or download the plain text result.

If important text is missing, inspect the source SSA file for `Comment:` rows, malformed `Dialogue:` rows, or unusual field order.

## Common mistakes

### Renaming SSA to TXT

Changing `captions.ssa` to `captions.txt` only changes the filename. It does not remove SSA metadata, timings, style definitions, or dialogue fields.

### Expecting timing to remain

TXT has no cue timing. If you need timed subtitles, convert SSA to SRT for uploads and editors, or convert SSA to VTT for browser players.

### Sending raw SSA to a translator

Raw SSA files bury the words inside dialogue rows and style syntax. Convert to TXT first when the task is translation, proofreading, or editorial review.

### Ignoring old encodings

Older SSA files may use Shift-JIS, Windows-1252, Big5, GB18030, or another legacy encoding. If names or CJK text look broken, fix the encoding before using the transcript.

## Frequently asked questions

### How do I convert SSA subtitles to TXT?

Open the [SSA to TXT Converter](/tools/ssa-to-txt/) with your `.ssa` file. It extracts readable dialogue text and removes SSA timing, style, and metadata fields.

### Will TXT keep SSA timing or styling?

No. Plain text keeps only readable dialogue. It removes timing, style sections, positions, fonts, colors, and effects.

### Can I upload TXT as subtitles?

No. TXT has no timing. Use [ASS to SRT Converter](/tools/ass-to-srt/) or [ASS to VTT Converter](/tools/ass-to-vtt/) when the output still needs to work as subtitles.
