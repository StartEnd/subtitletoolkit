ASS files can contain styling, positioning, effects, and metadata. None of that belongs in a plain text transcript.

## Quick answer

Use the [ASS to TXT Converter](/tools/ass-to-txt/) when you need the readable subtitle text from an `.ass` or `.ssa` file.

The converter reads dialogue lines, removes ASS override tags and timing fields, then outputs plain text.

## When this is useful

Convert ASS to TXT when:

- an editor exports styled subtitles but you only need the dialogue
- a translator needs text without style tags
- karaoke or position effects make the file hard to review
- you want a transcript draft from a styled subtitle file

If the destination is YouTube, Plex, or a simple upload workflow, convert ASS to SRT instead of TXT.

## Step-by-step workflow

1. Open the [ASS to TXT Converter](/tools/ass-to-txt/).
2. Upload the ASS or SSA file, or paste the subtitle text.
3. Convert the subtitle file to plain text.
4. Check that style tags such as `{\\an8}` or color overrides are gone.
5. Review line breaks created from ASS `\\N` line breaks.
6. Download the TXT output.

## What gets removed

ASS to TXT conversion removes:

- `[Script Info]`, `[V4+ Styles]`, and format sections
- dialogue timing and layer fields
- style names, margins, effects, and speaker metadata
- inline override tags for color, position, font, and animation

The goal is not to preserve design. The goal is readable text.

## Common mistakes

### Expecting styling to survive

TXT has no subtitle styling model. If you need simple timed captions, use the [ASS to SRT Converter](/tools/ass-to-srt/).

### Missing hard line breaks

ASS uses `\\N` for line breaks inside dialogue. After conversion, scan the text to make sure those breaks read naturally.

### Sending raw ASS to a writer

Raw ASS files are hard to review because dialogue is mixed with style syntax. Convert to TXT first for editorial work.
